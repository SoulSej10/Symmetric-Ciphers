How Playfair Works

The Playfair cipher is a digraph substitution cipher that encrypts pairs of letters using a 5x5 grid. The grid is filled with a keyword, followed by the remaining letters of the alphabet (excluding 'J'). The letters 'I' and 'J' are treated as the same letter.

For example, with the key "MONARCH", the grid might look like this:

            M O N A R
            C D E F G
            H I K L P
            Q S T U V
            W X Y Z B

To encrypt a message, the text is split into digraphs (pairs of letters). If there are repeating letters, an 'X' is inserted between them. The cipher follows these rules:

  • Same row: Replace with the letters immediately to the right.
  • Same column: Replace with the letters immediately below.
  • Different row and column: Form a rectangle and swap the letters at the opposite corners.

Decryption simply reverses these rules. The Playfair cipher is more secure than simpler ciphers but can still be broken with enough ciphertext.
